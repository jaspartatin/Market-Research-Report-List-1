<p><p>White dextrins and yellow dextrins are carbohydrate polymers derived from starch through the process of hydrolysis. White dextrins are lighter in color and have a higher molecular weight, making them suitable for applications in food products, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics due to their high solubility and low viscosity. Yellow dextrins, on the other hand, are more thermally stable, characterized by a slightly darker hue, and are commonly utilized in adhesives, textiles, and various industrial applications.</p><p>The White Dextrins and Yellow Dextrins Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.1% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by rising demand in the food and beverage sector, where these dextrins are used as bulking agents and stabilizers. Additionally, increasing applications in the pharmaceutical industry for drug formulations and in the paper and textile industries as binding agents are contributing to market expansion. The trend toward healthier food options is further bolstering the demand for white dextrins as a low-calorie alternative. Meanwhile, environmentally friendly products are gaining traction, encouraging innovations and sustainability within the dextrin manufacturing processes, which is likely to shape the market's future landscape.</p></p>
