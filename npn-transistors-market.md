<p><ul><li>Biopolar</li><li>Field-effect</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The NPN transistor market is primarily divided into bipolar and field-effect transistors (FETs). Bipolar NPN transistors rely on charge carriers (electrons and holes) for operation, offering high current gain and fast switching speeds, making them ideal for amplification and digital circuits. In contrast, field-effect NPN transistors, such as MOSFETs, control current through an electric field, providing high input impedance and lower power consumption. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to diverse needs in electronics and circuit design.</p></p>
