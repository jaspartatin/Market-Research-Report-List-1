<p><p>The baking chocolate market comprises several types, each catering to diverse culinary needs. Unsweetened chocolate contains pure cocoa solids and cocoa butter with no added sugar, ideal for recipes requiring full control over sweetness. Bittersweet chocolate has a higher sugar content and adds depth to baked goods. Semisweet chocolate, commonly found in chocolate chips, balances sweetness and bitterness. Sweet chocolate offers a significantly higher sugar level, suitable for desserts requiring richer flavor profiles. Each type serves distinct purposes in baking.</p></p>
